🆕 May 14 update — Ranch Roofing counter-offer + Sprague follow-up

Ranch Roofing dropped their price to $28,802 (from $31,152) and substantially increased the included rot allowance (now 144 LF / ~9 boards free, up from 64 LF / ~4 boards). Don from Ranch made several substantive points worth weighing:

  • PE ownership / warranty risk: GF Sprague was acquired in 2023 by Infinity Home Services, backed by LightBay Capital and Freeman Spogli & Co. The "Forever-Whoever" workmanship warranty's value depends on Sprague being around to honor it. Note: Mule-Hide's 15-yr product warranty is manufacturer-backed regardless, so material/membrane defects are covered either way — but workmanship coverage carries real PE-rollup risk. Ranch's Elevate Red Shield 20-yr full-system warranty is third-party backed (survives Ranch closing).
  • Roof drain: Ranch installs a new single-piece drain via licensed plumber; Sprague specifies a retrofit drain that inserts into the existing standpipe. Don's concern is that retrofit drains can leak through the threaded connection when backed up. Worth Sprague's response.
  • Rot allowance: At Ranch's new 144 LF free and $4/LF after, vs Sprague's 3 boards free and $14/LF after (capped at $4,028 for full deck) — if significant rot is found (4 existing layers to strip suggests it's plausible), Ranch closes ~$2,800 of the price gap.
  • Materials: Don argues Elevate > Mule-Hide and 1/2" HD Polyiso > 1" ISO. Real but mixed: Sprague's insulation is 2× thicker (more R-value), Ranch's is denser (more compression resistance). Roughly a wash.

New apples-to-apples gap: Ranch $28,802 vs Sprague $23,947 (roof + skylights, excluding Sprague's $498.78 front gutter that Ranch isn't quoting) = $4,855. Per-unit, ~$797–$1,219 more for Ranch.

⚠️ Important tax / incentive context for unit owners

  • Federal 30% solar tax credit (25D) expired 12/31/2025. Systems placed in service in 2026+ do not qualify. Several quotes still show stale federal credit numbers — ignore those.
  • MA Schedule SC credit is per principal residence: 15% of each owner's share of net cost, capped at $1,000 per unit. With 4 of 5 condo units owner-occupied, total MA credit lands around $2,940–$3,312 across the building.
  • Solar Insure 30-year blanket warranty covers panels, inverters, racking, labor, AND roof penetrations with $0 deductible. Critically, it covers installer/manufacturer insolvency — meaningful protection for a 30-year asset. Included in both front-runner quotes.

How to figure out your real rate from your Eversource bill

Don't just use the supplier's per-kWh rate (e.g. the Generation Service Charge, often around $0.149/kWh if you're on Cambridge Community Choice). That's only one piece of your cost.

The relevant rate for solar savings is the all-in variable cost per kWh, which is what you actually avoid paying when solar offsets a kWh:

  1. Find Subtotal Supplier Services (e.g. $88.06) and Subtotal Delivery Services (e.g. $66.09) on your bill.
  2. Add them together, then subtract the fixed Customer Charge (e.g. $9.57). That charge is flat regardless of usage — solar can't offset it.
  3. Divide by your kWh usage for the month (shown as "Current Usage", e.g. 591 kWh).

Example from a recent 109 Inman bill: ($88.06 + $66.09 − $9.57) ÷ 591 kWh ≈ $0.245/kWh. Use that number here.

Your rate may differ if you're on a different supplier (Eversource Basic Service is typically higher than CCC), a different rate class (R-1 vs R1HP heat pump), or have different monthly usage relative to the fixed customer charge.
