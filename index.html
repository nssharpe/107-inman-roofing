⚠️ Important context for unit owners

  • Federal 30% solar tax credit (25D) expired 12/31/2025. Systems placed in service in 2026+ do not qualify. Several quotes still show stale federal credit numbers in their financial projections — ignore those.
  • MA Schedule SC credit is per principal residence: 15% of each owner's share of net cost, capped at $1,000 per unit. With 4 of 5 condo units owner-occupied & eligible, total MA credit lands around $2,940–$3,312 across the building (under $1k cap at our quote prices).
  • Solar Insure 30-year blanket warranty covers panels, inverters, racking, labor, AND roof penetrations (within 3" of attachment) with $0 deductible. Critically, it covers installer/manufacturer insolvency — meaningful protection for a 30-year asset. Included in Boston Solar and Great Sky's "+Insure" options.

Open Questions Before Signing

  • Boston Solar: Walk through production-estimate methodology — 8,187 kWh is ~10% above the next-highest credible estimate on the same roof. Is it an Aurora shade study? Different soiling/snow assumptions?
  • Boston Solar: Confirm REC panel coverage track (Standard 20/25/0 vs ProTrust 25/25/25).
  • Great Sky Solar: What exactly does the +Solar Insure version add vs Standard for the $675 delta? (Standard already has 25-yr install warranty.)
  • Brightway Energy: Request workmanship warranty terms in writing.
  • Evergreen Solar: Identify the actual installer, their warranty terms, and reviews. Clarify whether the contract is with Evergreen or the installer (affects warranty enforcement and MA Schedule SC math).
  • All finalists: Confirm experience with MA condo / shared-roof installs (single contract vs. per-unit billing matters for the per-residence tax credit cap).
  • Tax advisor: Verify per-unit MA Schedule SC math given the shared-roof / multi-owner structure.

Recommendation

Site visits with Boston Solar and Great Sky Solar (+Solar Insure) — effectively tied on price, equipment, and warranty. Decision likely between these two; the deciding factor is the team you'd rather work with for a 30-year support relationship.

Boston Solar's edge: highest production estimate (worth understanding why), REC 460AA + IQ8X premium equipment, very large install volume.

Great Sky's edge: 1 mile away, never subcontracts, 5.0 across 968 reviews, no deposit/no binding contract until materials ordered.

Brightway Energy is the credible budget alternative if pushing down ~$1,400 is the priority — same neighborhood, premium panels (SEG BoB), but no whole-system warranty.

Evergreen Solar should not be evaluated further until they identify the actual installer behind their lead-gen proposal.
